# Supporting Articles

This folder contains background articles and explainers that help students understand the broader issue of fake news, misinformation, and online information credibility before beginning the technical workflow.

## Article 1: UNICEF — A quick guide to spotting misinformation

Link: https://www.unicef.org/eca/stories/quick-guide-spotting-misinformation

This article gives students a short and accessible introduction to misinformation. It explains how misleading information can spread online and gives practical guidance for evaluating what they see. This helps motivate the case study by showing why fake news detection matters beyond the machine learning model.

## Article 2: New Mexico State University — Fact Checking Online News and Social Media

Link: https://pubs.nmsu.edu/_g/G600.pdf

This printable PDF explains fake news, misinformation, disinformation, and strategies for checking online information. It is included as a practical background resource that students can use to better understand the real-world problem behind classifying news as real or fake.

## Why These Articles Are Included

These articles are included to help students understand the context of the case study before they start coding. The fake news classifier is not only a technical exercise; it also connects to media literacy, source evaluation, and the risks of misleading information online.
